<p style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;" dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-0509957c-bd91-edc7-94a7-cdec57e824c5"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Great news in Chameleon-land!</span></span></p>

<p><span id="docs-internal-guid-0509957c-bd91-edc7-94a7-cdec57e824c5"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">I hope everybody is enjoying the nice weather and hopefully a more relaxed schedule for the summer! Read on to find out what we’ve been up to last month.</span></span></p>

<p style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;" dir="ltr"> </p>

<p style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;" dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-0509957c-bd91-edc7-94a7-cdec57e824c5"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Work on deployment challenges.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> Many of you noticed recently that launching a large number of instances sometimes resulted in error; in addition, the system was occasionally slow or unresponsive, particularly on CHI@TACC which handles a larger number of nodes. We applied some patches and reconfigured the system to handle the load better. As a result the system is now stable again and the failure rates have dropped. We are still aware of some issues and are working on tracking down the last of them. Some issues will realistically persist. In particular, instances may still fail to launch due to unresponsive or broken hardware; this kind of issue is inherently linked to frequent bare-metal reconfiguration. For this reason, we still recommend that medium to large-scale experiments reserve slightly more nodes than needed, to account for a small percentage of failed launches.</span></span></p>

<p><span id="docs-internal-guid-0509957c-bd91-edc7-94a7-cdec57e824c5"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">New appliances and appliance upgrades.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> We made quite a few new appliances available this month to get you on your way faster with complex experiments. In addition to providing new functionality and upgrades, all of our new appliances also have the latest versions Chameleon tools installed, including cc-checks, cc-snapshot and Cloudfuse.</span></span></p>

<ul style="margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;">
	<li><span id="docs-internal-guid-0509957c-bd91-edc7-94a7-cdec57e824c5"><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">ExoGENI stitching appliance.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> </span><a style="text-decoration: none;" href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/55/"><span style="">This new appliance</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> automates the creation of a private VLAN network that can be stitched to the slices on the ExoGENI testbed. Simply launch the appliance and create an ExoGENI slice that includes a Chameleon stitchport to extend your experiment across both testbeds -- detailed instructions can be found </span><a style="text-decoration: none;" href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/docs/bare-metal-user-guide/stitching-chameleon-isolated-networks/"><span style="">here</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">.</span></span></li>
	<li><span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-0509957c-bd91-edc7-94a7-cdec57e824c5"><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Hadoop appliance. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">We created a </span><a style="text-decoration: none;" href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/53/"><span style="">Hadoop complex appliance</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> that deploys a one-click Hadoop cluster, including the Hadoop Distributed File System. The appliance can be used for most Hadoop experiments and can be easily scaled to an arbitrary number of nodes. The appliance comes with a tutorial (see appliance documentation) that teaches distributed computing and networking.</span></span></span></span></li>
	<li><span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-0509957c-bd91-edc7-94a7-cdec57e824c5"><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">CUDA appliance upgrades.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> We upgraded our CUDA appliance to support CUDA 9.1 for both CentOS 7 (</span><a style="text-decoration: none;" href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/52/"><span style="">CC-CentOS7-CUDA9</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">) and Ubuntu 16.04 (</span><a style="text-decoration: none;" href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/51/"><span style="">CC-Ubuntu16.04-CUDA9</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">); both can be launched at </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">CHI@TACC</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">. We will still provide full support of </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">CC-Ubuntu16.04-CUDA8</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">, but can provide only limited support for </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">CC-CentOS7-CUDA8</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> because CentOS 7 does not support CUDA 8.</span></span></span></span></span></span></li>
</ul>

<p style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;" dir="ltr"> </p>

<p style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;" dir="ltr"><span id="docs-internal-guid-0509957c-bd91-edc7-94a7-cdec57e824c5"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Changes in appliance charging.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> We have been seeing very high usage of our GPU, FPGA, and storage hierarchy nodes; GPU nodes in particular typically have to be reserved far in advance. Anticipating this usage dynamics, our </span><a style="text-decoration: none;" href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/news/happy-independence-day-chameleon/"><span style="">original intent</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> has been to eventually charge more SUs for their usage though we never enforced it in practice; we would like to start charging twice the regular SU rate to encourage experimenters to think twice about whether they really need those resources -- and if not make them available to others who do. The changes will come into effect on Monday.</span></span></p>

And </span><span id="docs-internal-guid-0509957c-bd91-edc7-94a7-cdec57e824c5"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: rgb(0, 0, 0); background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">as always, please let us know what you think of these changes. </span></span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Dear Chameleon users,</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">It's hard to believe, but we're once again approaching the tail end of summer, and the start of a new academic year. We've been busy with our "Summer on the Edge", but also have been getting back to our bare metal roots this month. Here's what we've got for you:</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Squashing bugs in python-chi. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">We really appreciate any </span><a href="https://github.com/ChameleonCloud/python-chi/issues?q=is%3Aissue+is%3Aclosed" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">bug reports</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> filed by users of </span><a href="https://python-chi.readthedocs.io/en/latest/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">python-chi</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, Chameleon's Python API library. To show our appreciation, we spent some time fixing these up for you all! As a reminder, python-chi is pre-installed on all Jupyter notebook servers in Chameleon's shared Jupyter environment, allowing you to perform all of your experiment management via Python/Jupyter.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><img src="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/media/filer_public/5c/c3/5cc37301-b443-4c64-bbba-60e9c01fa08e/pxfuelcom1.jpg" width="800"></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><em>Very interested in squashing bugs.</em></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Say cheese! Access camera devices on CHI@Edge. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">This month, we bought some </span><a href="https://www.raspberrypi.org/products/camera-module-v2/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Raspberry Pi camera modules</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> and plugged them into both our Raspberry Pi cameras and Jetson Nanos. While they're not pointed anywhere particularly interesting at the moment, they provide a great testbed environment for experiments involving video capture and streaming. This functionality, like most of CHI@Edge, is in preview and as such is only available via the Python interface; you can find an example in the </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/experiment/share/38" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">CHI@Edge user guide</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">; the example shows how to make a reservation for a device with a camera and launch a container that can access the camera device. We're working to expand the set of supported peripherals; we already support attaching the on-board GPUs to Jetson devices, and are working on more types of devices, like Raspberry Pi GPIO and generic USB devices.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Enroll x86 edge nodes on CHI@Edge. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Before this month, we had focused on the Jetson and Raspberry Pi platforms, targeting the Jetson Nano and Raspberry Pi 4 specifically. We're happy to announce that we've since expanded support for x86 edge nodes, allowing you to deploy edge nodes of many other standardized platforms.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Restrict edge device reservations to certain projects.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> When contributing an edge device to CHI@Edge, you may reasonably wonder: what if I and my colleagues need exclusive access for some time? As the device owner, we're making it possible to "lock down" an edge device such that it's only available to certain Chameleon projects. Please </span><a href="https://groups.google.com/g/chameleon-edge-users" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">ask us</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> if you want to do this, as the capability is only configurable by Chameleon operators; we plan to release it as part of device enrollment in the future.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Improved UI for complex reservations.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> Over time, we have made more resources on the testbed reservable: while we started with just bare metal nodes, we now support reserving VLANs, public IPs, and now edge devices in advance. The GUI for this functionality wasn't keeping up with the new capabilities, so we rewrote it to accommodate reservations spanning multiple types of resources. Give it a spin! It looks like this now:</span></p>

<p><img src="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/media/filer_public/1a/7e/1a7e19c6-51f4-4635-8bc2-f3dd32d673d2/screen_shot_2021-08-02_at_44729_pm.png" width="800"></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Get reminded when your allocation is about to expire. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">There's nothing worse than getting ready to do some really important work… only to find your Chameleon allocation has run out! We added a new email notification that the PI of the project will receive one month before the allocation will expire, to give you enough time to put in </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/user/project.html#recharge-or-extend-your-allocation" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">renewal or recharge</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> requests as applicable.</span></p>

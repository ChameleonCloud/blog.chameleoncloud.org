<p dir="ltr" id="docs-internal-guid-73a90565-7fff-6d04-aceb-c7880bf694c5" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Great news in Chameleon-land!</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">We have a few goodies below to start off the New Year on the right foot!</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">You may have noticed that our attention has been slowly shifting to upgrading and improving the system rather than extending it: we are not announcing new features as aggressively as at the beginning of phase 2 – but then we noticed that users often ask for features that have been in the system for a while or are not aware of tools like <a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/complex.html">orchestration</a> that can make their experimentation easier and more repeatable. Therefore, for the remainder of phase 3 we will shift focus onto “digesting” the capabilities we have, i.e., making them better, more reliable, and easier to use – but also better known via webinars and other outreach activities. This being the case, we will suspend the changelog posts for now and instead announce new features and upgrades as well as training opportunities via the users@chameleoncloud.org mailing list as they come up. Enjoy the new things that we did in January! </span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Simpler SDN experiments at UC. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Before, if you wanted to utilize </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_sdn.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">SDNs</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> in your experiment, you were limited to using the Skylake nodes, as they were the only nodes logically connected to a switch capable of creating isolated OpenFlow-ready networks (our Corsa DP2200/DP2400s). This limited the scale of your networking experiments. Now, that limitation is lifted: you can now additionally include Haswell nodes at CHI@UC participate in SDN experiments! We have updated the </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_sdn.html#chi-uc" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">documentation</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> to include the OpenFlow port mappings for each Haswell node. If you haven't already played with SDN and the </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_sdn.html#" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">BYOC</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> feature, perhaps now is the time? Check out this </span><a href="https://jupyter.chameleoncloud.org/import?source=github&amp;src_path=chameleoncloud/notebooks&amp;file_path=tutorials%2Fnetworking%2FBringYourOwnController.ipynb" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">example tutorial</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> (it will launch in the Chameleon Jupyter environment!)</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">New Jupyter tutorial notebook. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Speaking of Jupyter tutorials, we have another one, which devoted blog followers may have already taken notice of. This </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2020/01/30/accessing-multiple-nodes-private-network-without-dns-using-jupyter-notebook/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">tutorial</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> explores how you can orchestrate a distributed system and configure each node's DNS resolution (using /etc/hosts) such that you don't need to hard-code IP addresses in your experimental configuration: an important property for reproducibility (and for maintaining your own sanity). It also covers reserving multiple nodes, IP addresses, and creating isolated networks, all within Jupyter.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">KVM-2015 officially decommissioned. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">As announced, we have disabled the old KVM cloud in lieu of its </span><a href="https://kvm.tacc.chameleoncloud.org/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">more modern replacement</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. Please send us a message via a </span><a href="http://chameleoncloud.org/user/help/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">support ticket</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> if you find you need assistance with setting up your experiments on the new KVM cluster.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">System upgrades in February. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">We'll be performing some regular updates to the testbed in February, notably upgrading to the latest OpenStack installation (</span><a href="https://releases.openstack.org/train/highlights.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Train</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">), which includes a number of usability and performance improvements, as well as bug fixes. </span></p>

<p dir="ltr" id="docs-internal-guid-3bd9299d-7fff-b6f6-3d26-b2bfd2735d55" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Great news in Chameleon-land!</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Because you’re probably sick of turkey by now, we have something else to whet your appetite.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">OpenFlow experiments can now utilize multiple networks. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">We have deployed a new capability which allows users to associate multiple Chameleon OpenFlow networks to a single OpenFlow controller and switch abstraction. The centralized controller can be programed to selectively maintain network isolation or to forward traffic between networks based on the requirements of the experiment. This functionality works with both </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_vlan.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">localized isolated networks</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> as well as </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_stitching.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">externally (i.e. ExoGENI) stitchable network circuits</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. It enables advanced switching and routing experiments including </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2018/09/20/application-based-qos-support-p4-and-openflow/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">wide-area multipath routing</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. To find out more </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_sdn.html#corsa-dp2000-virtual-forwarding-contexts-network-layout-and-advanced-features" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">read our documentation</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. </span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2018/08/17/software-defined-networking-openflow-chameleon/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">BYOC (Bring Your Own Controller)</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> is now easier to do than ever</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. A common difficulty with using the OpenFlow BYOC capabilities has been the ability to know which OpenFlow switch port is connected to which node. A recent Chameleon update </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2018/10/01/chameleon-changelog-september-2018/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">standardized the mapping of port identifiers to the nodes at UC</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. We have now deployed this update at TACC as well and have created a similar standard mapping. The port mappings for both sites can be found in the </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_sdn.html#port-mapping" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">SDN networking documentation</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Temperature metrics for (all!) low power nodes are now available.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> In addition to the </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2018/07/02/chameleon-changelog-june-2018/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">power metrics announced in June</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, Chameleon now collects temperature metrics for all low power nodes (Intel Atom, Low Power Xeon, and ARM64 Nodes) via the `temperature_cpu` metric. Take a look at our </span><a href="http://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/metrics.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">docs</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> to see an example of how to use this new metric, and if you need a refresher or have not used this feature before, we have a </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2018/06/22/monitoring-power-consumption-low-power-nodes/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">blog post</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> covering the original feature.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">KVM cloud traces for past 4 years available for analysis. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Making cloud traces available is critical to supporting research as it enables researchers to validate their research by testing how it would perform on real life data. We updated the existing Chameleon OpenStack KVM cloud traces on </span><a href="https://scienceclouds.org/cloud-traces/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Science Clouds</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> to include data for the past 4 years. As part of this, we now also provide the </span><a href="https://github.com/ChameleonCloud/starcompactor" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">software tool</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> that we used to extract the data and a </span><a href="https://github.com/ChameleonCloud/starcompactor/blob/master/ChameleonKVMCloudTraces.ipynb" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Jupyter Notebook example</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> illustrating how one can share traces from similar OpenStack clouds easily. For more information about cloud traces, please visit our </span><a href="https://scienceclouds.org/cloud-traces/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">cloud traces page on Science Clouds</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> and </span><a href="https://press3.mcs.anl.gov/scienceclouds/contact/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">contact us</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> if you would like to either use our data or contribute your own.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">(Even) better Chameleon Jupyter Notebooks. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Last month </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2018/11/01/chameleon-changelog-october-2018/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">we announced</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> the introduction of a shared </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/swift.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Swift</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">-backed drive to the JupyterLab application, which allows you to share Notebooks with collaborators within your project. This month we improved the reliability of that, especially concerning larger Notebook files, and also added an ability to switch the active project for those of you who are affiliated with multiple projects on Chameleon--see </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/jupyter.html#shared-swift-drive" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">here</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> for instructions. If you’re new to Jupyter, or just want to know more about how to share your research with collaborators using the Notebook as a convenient medium, we have prepared </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/jupyter.html#notebook-collaboration" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">some docs</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> for you.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Usability improvements.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> Some users noticed that when they were signed in to the wrong testbed region, clicking a link to launch an appliance from the </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">appliance catalog</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> would give you an uninformative 404 error. Those links got a talking to and won’t do that anymore. We also added a way to filter appliances for only those officially supported by Chameleon staff, so you can choose to only use images we actively keep an eye on for misbehavior.</span></p>

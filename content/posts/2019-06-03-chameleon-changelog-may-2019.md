<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Great news in Chameleon-land!</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">It’s summertime, and that means open-flame grills, pools, and hot temperatures: all things that our servers absolutely hate, the poor things. Fortunately they get to stay cool in our data center, and even more fortunately, they even got some nice upgrades.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">100G networking at CHI@NU now ready for experimentation.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> Back in October </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2018/11/01/chameleon-changelog-october-2018/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">we announced</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> a new Chameleon Associate Site at Northwestern. The Northwestern site has a few specialized nodes, notably nodes with a Mellanox ConnectX-5 100GE card, allowing for 100G experimentation on a single node. However, up until now, that node wasn’t able to take advantage of these high bandwidths on paths in or out of the testbed. As of this month, these nodes are now connected to 100G-capable uplinks, allowing you to connect the Northwestern site to other Chameleon sites or academic institutions (via ExoGENI stitching). Access to the CHI@NU site is still on an as-needed basis, so if you are interested please</span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/user/help/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> </span><span style="">open a ticket via the helpdesk</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> to request access and  include “CHI@NU” in the subject of the ticket to help us expedite your request. Once your account on the new site has been activated, you will be able to reach the site at</span><a href="https://sl-ciab.northwestern.edu/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> </span><span style="">https://sl-ciab.northwestern.edu</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">More disk space on P100 GPU nodes.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> In response to many user requests, our P100 GPU nodes at TACC have been upgraded from 250GB to 1TB of storage space! We hope that this improved capacity assists you in experimenting with larger datasets and models on these nodes. Side note: did you know Chameleon has over 2PB of internal storage available via the </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/swift.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Object Store</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">? It’s a good place to store experimental artifacts you might need closer to your bare metal nodes, like large data sets or trained models. There is a FUSE mount conveniently located on any instance you launch with Chameleon; learn how to use it </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/swift.html#mounting-object-store-as-a-file-system" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">here</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Provision layer-2 circuits with one command!</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> If you’re already familiar with </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_stitching.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">stitchable VLANs</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> via ExoGENI, you’ve probably been interacting with the </span><a href="http://www.exogeni.net/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">GENI tools</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> to provision those links. There can be a lot of steps to this, and it’s been our goal to provide a simpler way to do this. That day has come! There is a binary tool, available in preview in our </span><a href="https://jupyter.chameleoncloud.org/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">JupyterHub</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> environment, that allows you to provision circuits with one command using your GENI credentials. You can check it out in our </span><a href="https://jupyter.chameleoncloud.org/hub/user-redirect/lab/tree/notebooks/tutorials/networking/Tutorial-DirectStitch.ipynb" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">DirectStitch tutorial</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Speaking of tutorials… </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">in March </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2019/04/01/chameleon-changelog-march-2019/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">we announced</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> some new tutorials you could take as part of our </span><a href="https://jupyter.chameleoncloud.org/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">JupyterHub environment</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. We’ve added to our set of tutorials substantially in May, in particular covering </span><a href="https://jupyter.chameleoncloud.org/hub/user-redirect/lab/tree/notebooks/tutorials/getting-started/Orchestration.ipynb" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">orchestration</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, </span><a href="https://jupyter.chameleoncloud.org/hub/user-redirect/lab/tree/notebooks/tutorials/getting-started/DataManagement.ipynb" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">data management</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> (including snapshotting and use of the Object Store), the </span><a href="https://jupyter.chameleoncloud.org/hub/user-redirect/lab/tree/notebooks/tutorials/getting-started/CommandLineInterface.ipynb" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">command-line interface</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; Check them out when you have a moment! We also freshened up our </span><a href="https://jupyter.chameleoncloud.org/hub/user-redirect/lab/tree/notebooks/tutorials/getting-started/IntroductionToChameleon.ipynb" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">introduction to Chameleon</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> tutorial, which could be helpful for any students or members of your group who are new to the testbed.</span></p>

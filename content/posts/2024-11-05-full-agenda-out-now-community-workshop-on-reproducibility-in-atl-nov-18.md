<article class="blog-post">We're excited to announce the full agenda for the upcoming Community Workshop on Practical Reproducibility in HPC, taking place on November 18, 2024, at Terminus 330 in Atlanta, GA. Visit the <a href="https://reproduciblehpc.org">workshop website</a> for complete details.
<div class="post-content">
<h2>Agenda at a Glance</h2>

<div class="agenda-summary">
<ul>
	<li><strong>8:30 AM - 9:00 AM:</strong> Welcome Reception &amp; Breakfast</li>
	<li><strong>9:00 AM - 10:00 AM:</strong> Opening Remarks and Morning Keynote by Dr. Kate Keahey</li>
	<li><strong>10:30 AM - 12:30 PM:</strong> Morning Presentations/Panel Session</li>
	<li><em><strong>Lunch Break</strong></em></li>
	<li><strong>1:30 PM - 2:30 PM:</strong> Afternoon Keynote by Dr. Torsten Hoefler</li>
	<li><strong>3:00 PM - 5:00 PM:</strong> Afternoon Presentations/Panel Session</li>
	<li><strong>5:00 PM - 6:30 PM:</strong> Concluding Remarks &amp; Rooftop Happy Hour</li>
</ul>

<p class="agenda-note">View the <a href="https://reproduciblehpc.org/#agenda">complete detailed agenda</a> on the workshop website.</p>
</div>

<h2>Keynote Speakers</h2>
We are honored to feature two distinguished keynote speakers:

<ul>
	<li><strong>Dr. Kate Keahey</strong> (Argonne National Laboratory/UChicago) will present "Adaptable Infrastructures for Reproducible Science - The Chameleon 4 Approach", exploring how Chameleon's latest iteration advances reproducible research infrastructure</li>
	<li><strong>Dr. Torsten Hoefler</strong> (ETH Zürich) will discuss "Reproducing Performance - The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly", addressing the unique challenges of performance reproducibility in HPC systems</li>
</ul>

<h2>Session Chairs</h2>
The workshop sessions will be chaired by distinguished leaders in HPC reproducibility:

<ul>
	<li><strong>Dr. Tanu Malik</strong> (DePaul University), former SC21 Reproducibility Chair, brings extensive experience in artifact evaluation and reproducibility initiatives</li>
	<li><strong>Dr. Sascha Hunold</strong> (TU Wien), current SC24 Reproducibility Chair, is leading innovations in reproducibility practices for the supercomputing community</li>
</ul>

<h2>Featured Presenters</h2>
We're delighted to showcase presentations from leading researchers in the field:

<ul>
	<li><strong>Tanu Malik</strong> presenting a comprehensive analysis of the SC21 Reproducibility Initiative and its impact on artifact evaluation processes</li>
	<li><strong>Sam Grayson</strong> (University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign) discussing testbed packaging for reproducibility workflows</li>
	<li><strong>Ruben Laso</strong> (University of Vienna) exploring C++ multicore and GPU benchmark reproducibility</li>
	<li><strong>Triveni Gurram</strong> and <strong>David Koop</strong> (Northern Illinois University) presenting on visualization reproducibility in HPC</li>
	<li><strong>Akhilesh Raj</strong> (Vanderbilt University) addressing performance and power optimization strategies</li>
	<li><strong>Klaus Kraßnitzer</strong> (IST Austria) introducing AutoAppendix for one-click reproducibility</li>
	<li><strong>Bogdan Stoica</strong> (University of Chicago) analyzing scalability bugs in large-scale systems</li>
	<li><strong>Kevin Kostage</strong> (Florida Gulf Coast University) presenting on federated learning using Chameleon</li>
	<li><strong>Adithya Raman</strong> (University at Buffalo) discussing energy consumption metrics for reproducibility</li>
	<li><strong>Quentin Guilloteau</strong> (University of Basel) sharing insights on artifact evaluation experiences</li>
</ul>

<h2>Schedule Highlights</h2>

<ul>
	<li>Morning and afternoon presentation sessions featuring speakers from leading institutions</li>
	<li>Interactive panel discussions with presenters</li>
	<li>Networking opportunities during breaks</li>
	<li>Concluding rooftop happy hour with stunning Atlanta views</li>
</ul>

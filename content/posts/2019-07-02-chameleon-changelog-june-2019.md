<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">The Stones are on tour, a TV star is the President, and your refrigerator can talk to you. If you feel temporally untethered, rest assured that there’s always something you can count on, month after month: a Chameleon changelog.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">One quick note: there are only 15 spots for our advanced networking tutorial using ExoGENI and Jupyter Notebooks! Register </span><a href="https://renci.zoom.us/webinar/register/WN_3a9KOe_ZTDqZQrIngobW-g" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">here</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Additional security measures available for bare metal instances. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">As announced last week on the </span><a href="https://lists.chameleoncloud.org/mailman/listinfo/users" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Users mailing list</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, we have rolled out a configurable firewall at both of our bare metal sites, CHI@TACC and CHI@UC. This “Firewall as a Service” enables you to define your own custom firewall rules to protect your experimental networks (or otherwise influence the ingress and egress traffic on your </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_vlan.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">isolated networks</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">.) As part of this, we will be enabling a set of </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_basic.html#firewall" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">default firewall rules</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> on the “sharednet1” and “sharedwan1” networks on </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">July 8</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. This firewall will prevent most traffic from the public Internet from reaching your experimental environment (some default services like SSH and HTTP(s) will be allowed; if you would like us to consider enabling additional services by default please email us!) You can re-use this default firewall policy on your own networks if you want! Have a gander at the </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_vlan.html#adding-a-firewall" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">relevant documentation section</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> to learn more about how to use this new feature.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Ubuntu 18.04 support has arrived.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> The Bionic Beaver is finally here! Chameleon now has an official appliance for the latest Ubuntu distribution. This </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/69/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">CC-Ubuntu18.04</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> appliance can be launched at CHI@UC, CHI@TACC, and KVM@TACC. As with all of the Chameleon-supported appliances, it comes with the latest versions of Chameleon tools installed, including cc-checks, cc-snapshot and cc-cloudfuse. Now that the 18.04 LTS version is supported on Chameleon, we will be phasing out support for Ubuntu 14.04, which had upstream support end in April of this year. As a general rule, we aim to officially support the last two LTS versions of Ubuntu for you.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">CUDA 10: now with even more CUDA in every box! </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">We also upgraded our CUDA appliance to support CUDA 10.1 for both CentOS 7 (</span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/68/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">CC-CentOS7-CUDA10</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">) and Ubuntu 16.04 (</span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/67/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">CC-Ubuntu16.04-CUDA10</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">); support for Ubuntu 18.04 will be coming hot on the heels of our release of an official Ubuntu 18.04 appliance this month. These upgraded CUDA appliances can be launched on the GPU-enabled hardware at CHI@TACC. While we will still provide full support of </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/52/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">CC-CentOS7-CUDA9</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, we can provide only limited support for the older CUDA 8 appliances. We encourage you to use the </span><a href="https://lists.chameleoncloud.org/mailman/listinfo/users" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Users mailing list</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> to ask questions about these appliances to other Chameleon users!</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Great news in Chameleon-land!</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">The turkey has turned, the online deals have soured, but there’s always one thing you can count on to not go bad: a Chameleon changelog.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Cascade Lake hardware available for all users! </span><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Newly added to CHI@TACC are 24 Dell PowerEdge R740 nodes equipped with </span><a href="https://ark.intel.com/content/www/us/en/ark/products/192440/intel-xeon-gold-6242-processor-22m-cache-2-80-ghz.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Intel Cascade Lake</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> CPUs, 192 GB RAM, and a 240GB SSD. These nodes have taken the place of several of our older Haswell-based nodes and are available for reservations immediately! The cost in </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/project.html#service-units" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">SUs</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> is the same as the Haswell or Skylake compute nodes. Check out the detailed specifications in our </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/hardware/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">hardware catalog</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Version support for shared artifacts. </span><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">In September we </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2019/10/03/chameleon-changelog-september-2019/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">announced</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> the Sharing Portal: a collection of, and more importantly a mechanism for contributing, experimental artifacts for others to reuse, redeploy, and reconsider. This month we have added support for versioned artifacts. Each artifact you publish will receive a DOI from </span><a href="https://zenodo.org/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Zenodo</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> and is considered “frozen” at that point, so that it can be reliable reference in the future. However, sometimes you may desire to publish a new version, if your artifact is e.g., a library, code sample, or reference implementation. Perhaps you simply wish to correct some errata you found after publishing the initial version. In any case, you can upload a new set of files (whatever they may be) and publish a new version of your existing artifact from your Jupyter server with the click of a button. The new version will be semantically linked to the prior versions to ensure the history is not lost. Check out our </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/jupyter/jupyter_sharing.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">docs</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> for more information about the Sharing Portal!</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">A more streamlined way to launch instances. </span><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">When launching bare metal instances via the Chameleon GUI, it was always required to </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/baremetal.html#id6" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">select the “baremetal” flavor</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> explicitly. Keeping with our focus on bare metal provisioning, we’ve made this the default selection, removing another step from the process of launching an instance! We also made the disk image selection screen load a looooot faster for you.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Reminder: old KVM cloud approaching end-of-life in January. </span><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Last month we </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2019/11/05/chameleon-changelog-october-2019/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">announced</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> the introduction of a </span><a href="https://kvm.tacc.chameleoncloud.org/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">new KVM deployment</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, which includes several stability improvements. We plan to decommission the </span><a href="https://openstack.tacc.chameleoncloud.org" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">old KVM cloud</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> in January 2020. Please work to migrate your workloads to the new environment; in many cases, this should be as simple as switching URLs. See our </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/kvm.html#migrating-from-kvm-2015" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">documentation</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> for more information on the migration, and </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/user/help/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">let us know</span></a><span style="font-size: 11.5pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> if you encounter issues.</span></p>

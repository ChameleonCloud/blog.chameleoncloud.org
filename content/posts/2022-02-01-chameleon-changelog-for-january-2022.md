<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Dear Chameleon users,</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">It's been a hard-ware month</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> (sung to the tune of that helpful Beatles ditty)! We've got some great updates about new hardware, some provided by an entirely new Chameleon site. Without further ado:</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">A brand new Chameleon site is online!</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> We are very happy to welcome a new associate site to Chameleon, increasing the total amount and diversity of hardware available to all researchers.</span> <a href="https://ncar.ucar.edu/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">NCAR</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> (National Center for Atmospheric Research) in Boulder, Colorado and is now the home of CHI@NCAR; this site evolved out of the IndySCC competition last year, and now will continue to serve the computer science research community by donating their unique and high-powered ARM nodes: the site is equipped with 5 </span><a href="https://www.gigabyte.com/us/Enterprise/ARM-Server/R181-T92-rev-100" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Cavium ARM Thunder X2</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> nodes, each with 2 CPUs, a total of 64 cores (256 threads), and 128GB RAM. The site can be reached here: </span><a href="https://chi.hpc.ucar.edu/project/leases/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">CHI@NCAR</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Node descriptions for are available in the </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/hardware/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">resource catalogue</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. Enjoy, and join me in giving the site operators a hand for making this a reality!</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Heavy-hitting AMD nodes installed at CHI@TACC!</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> More new hardware? Indeed! CHI@TACC has installed 20 new nodes, each with two AMD EPYC 7763 and a total of 128 cores (256 threads), 256 GB RAM and 480GB SSD storage. These nodes are divided into two groups: the first 16 are ready for a future upgrade with Mellanox HDR 100 Gb/s InfiniBand (we will announce when this has arrived and is configured, supply chain availability is causing significant delays); the remaining 4 have been configured with two AMD MI100 GPUs, which is available today.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Increased storage and GPU opportunities at CHI@UC!</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> These hardware updates are getting a bit much, no? At CHI@UC, several existing nodes have been upgraded with additional hardware. In particular, we've installed 8 SSDs (with a mixture of consumer and enterprise grade). </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/hardware/node/sites/uc/clusters/chameleon/nodes/6f8e4223-5141-479d-a452-5f4484cdaeb8" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Two</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/hardware/node/sites/uc/clusters/chameleon/nodes/9a3d528e-b7ed-407e-bbf0-4bac74161204" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">nodes</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> have been upgraded with 2x </span><a href="https://ark.intel.com/content/www/us/en/ark/products/205382/intel-ssd-d7p5510-series-7-68tb-2-5in-pcie-4-0-x4-3d4-tlc.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Intel 7.68TB PCIe4.0 SSDs</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, and </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/hardware/node/sites/uc/clusters/chameleon/nodes/4ad28a72-c19d-47d1-ae09-e54b77ed39d1" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">two</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/hardware/node/sites/uc/clusters/chameleon/nodes/7ed407a7-98dd-4708-bf32-9e0ab50c9f68" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">others</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. This means that many experiments needing more storage capacity or performing experiments on storage itself are now possible. Finally, we installed another V100 GPU that arrived late to the party to </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/hardware/node/sites/uc/clusters/chameleon/nodes/24401231-4587-4377-a5ff-cc8b51ab99ac" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">one of the Phase 3 nodes</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, increasing total GPU node capacity.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">CHI-in-a-Box stable release</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. During the rollout of the new associate sites, we've been hard at work on kicking the tires, working out the kinks, and putting a layer of polish (or clear-coat, I guess, to continue with the vaguely automotive metaphor) to </span><a href="https://github.com/ChameleonCloud/chi-in-a-box" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">CHI-in-a-Box</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, the packaging of the CHameleon Infrastructure (CHI), which is powering all Chameleon sites. Over the past month we've been improving on the installation process with feedback from our two new sites and </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.gitbook.io/chi-in-a-box" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">documenting a lot more information and options</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. Notably, we've added support for </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.gitbook.io/chi-in-a-box/reference/ironic-flat-networking" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">"flat" networking</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, where CHI does not need management access to the network fabric and can use pre-allocated network segments instead. This does mean users of the site cannot provision their own isolated L2 networks, but can accommodate more restrictive host environments.</span></p>

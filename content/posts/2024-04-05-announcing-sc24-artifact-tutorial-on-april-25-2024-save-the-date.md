<p><strong>When &amp; Where</strong>: Join us on April 25th, 2024, from 10 AM to 12 PM CT for a Zoom webinar (<a href="https://uchicago.zoom.us/webinar/register/WN_dMwxs5FmTUKwgje4ow47bw#/registration">registration link</a>) designed to navigate the complexities and unlock the full potential of the Chameleon testbed for high-performance computing (HPC) research.</p>

<p><strong>Who Should Attend</strong>: Whether you are new to Chameleon or have dabbled in its vast capabilities, this session promises valuable insights for all HPC researchers and authors focused on SC24. With a curriculum that bridges foundational knowledge and advanced techniques, participants will leave equipped to leverage Chameleon for groundbreaking research. The tutorial is an opportunity to streamline your research workflow, ensuring your work is efficient, reproducible, and impactful.</p>

<p><strong>Don’t Miss Out</strong>: Advance your research and ensure your experiments stand out at SC24. <strong><a href="https://uchicago.zoom.us/webinar/register/WN_dMwxs5FmTUKwgje4ow47bw#/registration">Register now</a></strong> to secure your spot in this essential tutorial and take the first step towards mastering Chameleon for high-performance computing research.</p>

<p><strong>Tutorial Overview</strong>:</p>

<ul>
	<li><strong>Introduction to Chameleon &amp; Support Resources</strong>: Start with a comprehensive overview of the Chameleon testbed. Learn what the testbed offers, how to navigate the interface, set up your environment, and where to find the array of support resources designed to facilitate your research projects. This segment ensures a smooth initiation into using Chameleon for your experiments.</li>
	<li><strong>Experiment Orchestration</strong>: Dive into orchestrating experiments, focusing on utilizing the Chameleon CLI, <a href="https://python-chi.readthedocs.io/en/latest/">python-chi library</a>, and GUI for a comprehensive management of Chameleon's resources. Learn to navigate hardware specifications, manage node availability, customize and deploy images, and configure network settings efficiently. This section highlights essential strategies to optimize resource usage and minimize waste, ensuring your experiments are as effective as possible.</li>
	<li><s><strong>Overview for HPC Experiments</strong></s>
	<ul>
		<li><s><strong>Effective Data Management</strong>: Efficiently managing data is crucial in HPC research. We'll cover strategies for importing and exporting data on Chameleon, emphasizing practices that enhance project efficiency and scalability.</s></li>
		<li><s><strong>Leveraging MPI and CUDA</strong>: For researchers looking to utilize parallel computing or GPU-accelerated applications, this tutorial provides an introduction to using MPI and CUDA on Chameleon. Enhance the performance of your computational experiments by mastering these tools.</s></li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li><strong>Sharing Artifacts via Trovi</strong>: We’ll explore how to maximize your research's impact by sharing artifacts on <a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/sharing.html">Trovi</a>. This platform facilitates the sharing, discovery, and replication of scientific artifacts, a cornerstone for collaborative and reproducible research.</li>
	<li><strong>Interactive Q&amp;A Session</strong>: Pose your questions and scenarios to Chameleon operators for personalized advice, deepening your understanding of the testbed’s capabilities.</li>
</ul>

<p>Questions? For further information or inquiries about the tutorial, please don’t hesitate to contact us at <a href="mailto:mtrichardson@uchicago.edu">mtrichardson@uchicago.edu</a>. We’re here to assist and ensure you have all the information needed to benefit fully from this session.</p>

We will send out an additional announcement when we have a date for the second section. Thank you for your understanding!</p>

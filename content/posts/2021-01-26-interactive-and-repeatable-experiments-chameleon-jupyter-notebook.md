<p dir="ltr">As another semester begins, we’ve rounded up a series of fully packaged experiments on <a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/">Chameleon</a>. These experiments are all publicly available on <a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/experiment/share/">Trovi</a>, Chameleon’s sharing platform. The experiments can be used for classes to introduce different topics, tools, or datasets, or serve as an introduction to provisioning resources on <a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/jupyter.html">Chameleon with Jupyter Notebook</a>. Once you launch an experiment, you can edit the notebook, allowing individual experimentation, and letting you introduce variation, such as trying different resources or datasets. </p>

<p>The structure used in the example notebooks to provision and launch resources can also be adapted to your needs, saving time to help you experiment! Simply copy the relevant commands to a notebook of your choosing, and begin your own experiments. </p>

<p>Read on to learn more about the experiments currently available, how to access them, and what you need to do to begin experimenting! At the end of this blog, you’ll find steps, complete with pictures, to guide you through launching an experiment on <a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/experiment/share/">Trovi</a> with <a href="https://jupyter.org/">Jupyter Notebook</a>.</p>

<p> </p>

<p dir="ltr"><b id="docs-internal-guid-4ab82a46-7fff-1172-66dc-490361dce7d9">Launching an Experiment from Trovi: All You Need to Know</b></p>

<p dir="ltr">One of the experiments has piqued your interest, now what? All it takes is 3 simple steps to get started experimenting!</p>

<ol>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">After you click on any of the packaged notebook links mentioned in this blog, you’ll be taken to its shared <a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/experiment/share/">Trovi</a> page. From here, click ‘Launch on Chameleon’. If you aren’t logged into Chameleon, you’ll have to log in, but then the Jupyter Notebook will begin to load.</p>
	</li>
</ol>

<p dir="ltr" style="text-align: center;"><b id="docs-internal-guid-4ab82a46-7fff-1172-66dc-490361dce7d9"><img height="345" src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/OFq8LtVAySlD8AQZksc_60OmW2Zc4eepuGPT3nXyvAM-8ilmH3b2IxCr29YoT_VpCbYjqZQPN5NA50YBZA_erLb7GOUF6qrMwMzUXmIiN5E5hcMtn8lVtVkFN4GNBfeDTPkweecg" width="624"></b></p>

<ol start="2">
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">Once the Jupyter Notebook loads, you’ll see all the files associated with that experiment. On some experiments, you might have to click through folders first to navigate to the folder with the experimental files Most of the experiments contain a reservation script, GPU or CPU setup scripts, a yaml setup file, the experiment in a python file, and <code>run_experiment</code> and analysis python notebooks. </p>
	</li>
	<li dir="ltr">
	<p dir="ltr">To run the experiment, click on the Python notebook labeled as such (a variation of <code>run_experiment</code>), and begin running cells. </p>
	</li>
</ol>

<p>We're excited to announce that <a href="https://www.anl.gov/profile/valerie-e-taylor"><strong>Valerie Taylor</strong></a>, Director of the Mathematics and Computer Science Division and Distinguished Fellow at Argonne National Laboratory, will deliver the keynote address at the Sixth Chameleon User Meeting, taking place <strong>April 15–16, 2026</strong> at NCAR's Mesa Lab in Boulder, Colorado.</p>

<p><img src="https://chameleoncloud.org/media/filer_public/3d/9c/3d9c01c4-18dc-4251-8c20-4ab247c4a3a0/valerie_taylor.jpg"></p>

<p>Dr. Taylor's talk, <em>Utilizing LLMs for Refactoring of Parallel Scientific Codes for Energy Efficiency</em>, will present LASSI-EE — an automated LLM-based framework that generates energy-efficient parallel code through runtime power profiling, energy-aware prompting, and self-correcting feedback. Results include benchmarks run on NVIDIA A100 and AMD MI100 GPUs available on Chameleon Cloud, with energy efficiency improvements of nearly 3x over standard LLM prompting.</p>

<p>Dr. Taylor is an IEEE Fellow, ACM Fellow, and AAAS Fellow, and also serves as CEO and President of <a href="https://cmd-it.org/">CMD-IT</a>. Her work sits at the intersection of high-performance computing and applied AI — a natural fit for this year's meeting theme.</p>

<p><strong>Join us in Boulder.</strong> View the full program and session details on the <a href="https://chameleoncloud.org/chameleon-cloud-users-meeting/sixth-chameleon-user-meeting/">event website</a>, and register via <a href="https://www.eventbrite.com/e/6th-chameleon-user-meeting-tickets-1982009337282">Eventbrite</a>.</p>

<p>And if you're arriving early, join us for a <strong>Pre-Meeting Hike on April 14th at 4:30 PM</strong> — a chance to explore the Boulder foothills and connect with fellow attendees before the meeting kicks off.</p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span id="docs-internal-guid-cd0e79a8-7fff-afed-1ba3-4b9f984dc0cf" style=""><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Great news in Chameleon-land!</span></span></p>

<p><span id="docs-internal-guid-cd0e79a8-7fff-afed-1ba3-4b9f984dc0cf" style=""><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">To start the New Year off on the right foot we added some new mouth watering features for you to try!</span></span></p>

<p><span id="docs-internal-guid-cd0e79a8-7fff-afed-1ba3-4b9f984dc0cf" style=""><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">VLAN reservations.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> It has now been a year since Chameleon started to support </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_stitching.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">stitching isolated layer 2 networks to ExoGENI VLANs</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">, or to other networks connected to the Internet2 Advanced Layer 2 Service (AL2S). This feature has been very popular with our users, to the point that the limited pool of stitchable VLANs on each Chameleon site was regularly fully used. To make sure that you can rely on the availability of a stitchable VLAN for your conference deadline or your demo, we made VLANs an allocatable resource, i.e., a resource that you can </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/reservations.html#creating-a-lease-to-reserve-a-vlan-segment" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">reserve in advance</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> and </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/reservations.html#the-lease-calendars" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">track the availability of via a lease calendar</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">. In the same way physical nodes can be reserved via Blazar, you can now create a lease to reserve a VLAN segment. This new feature is initially rolled out for a limited set of VLANs and will be gradually extended to cover all stitchable VLANs. Local VLANs can still be allocated directly without creating a reservation. To find out more, read </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/reservations.html#creating-a-lease-to-reserve-a-vlan-segment" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">our documentation</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">!</span></span></p>

<p><span id="docs-internal-guid-cd0e79a8-7fff-afed-1ba3-4b9f984dc0cf" style=""><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Automated Deployment. </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">We have enabled a new feature on Chameleon: </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/complex.html#automated-deployment" style="text-decoration: none;">Advanced Deployment</a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> allows you to automatically launch an experiment configured with Heat as soon as your CHI reservation starts — so that if your GPU reservation happens to start at 3 AM in the morning you can sleep peacefully in the knowledge that your automated Heat template will start configuring your lease as soon as it becomes available and your experiment will be ready and running when you show up in the office several hours later. For now, this feature has only been rolled out at UC — we will enable it on the TACC site later. To make the most effective use of this feature first learn </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/complex.html#" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">how to manage complex experiments with Heat</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> and then read the documentation on </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/complex.html#automated-deployment" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">how to have it triggered by your advance reservation</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">. </span></span></p>

<p><span id="docs-internal-guid-cd0e79a8-7fff-afed-1ba3-4b9f984dc0cf" style=""><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Usability improvements.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> It used to be that users would have to log in to two separate web interfaces in Chameleon: one allowed you to access your user profile and the other various testbed functions like lease creation. While manageable, this posted an additional barrier between you and your experiment. This barrier has now been removed: all of Chameleon’s web interactions are now accessible via a single login. Together with the </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2018/07/02/chameleon-changelog-june-2018/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">multi-region configuration we announced in June</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> which gives you single login to both UC and TACC, this feature makes accessing Chameleon resources easier than ever. </span></span></p>

<p><span id="docs-internal-guid-cd0e79a8-7fff-afed-1ba3-4b9f984dc0cf" style=""><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Better network configuration == more experiments.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> You may have noticed that we were doing some networking maintenance on the UC site this month. We are happy to announce we’ve completed the installation of a new spine switch at UC which will enable you to run more complex networking experiments there. In particular, previously in order to run  </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_sdn.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">BYOC (Bring Your Own Controller) experiments</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> you had to request one of the highly in-demand </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_stitching.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">stitchable VLANs</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; background-color: transparent; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">. Now you can use any local VLAN -- and there are many more of them which means less contention. We hope this change both simplifies things for you and also allows you to create more interesting network topologies.</span></span></p>

<p>This is it for now -- we are hoping to see all of you at the User Meeting next week!</p>

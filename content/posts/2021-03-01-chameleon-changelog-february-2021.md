<p dir="ltr" id="docs-internal-guid-85fc2a03-7fff-a7e9-fb76-76aac169ef74" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Dear Chameleon users,</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Welcome to a new month, full of possibility and intrigue! In the Northern Hemisphere we anxiously await the vanquishment of our common enemy, "Wintry Mix." In the southern hemisphere, I'm not sure what you're up to because I've been too hesitant to look. One can only take so much contrast. In any event, here's what we've been up to whilst waiting out the end of our winter.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">A new major release of CHI-in-a-Box.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> </span><a href="https://github.com/chameleoncloud/chi-in-a-box" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">CHI-in-a-Box</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> is the packaging of Chameleon--a set of software and provisioning tools that allows anybody to stand up their own Chameleon site. Over the last months we have been working on a new release that eliminates some major shortcomings. CHI-in-a-Box now includes project and account management via Chameleon's federated identity, and utilization accounting. We also vastly improved the documentation and tooling, wrote a new </span><a href="https://github.com/ChameleonCloud/chi-in-a-box/wiki/Installation-guide" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Installation Guide</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, fixed several bugs, and added support for CentOS 8 on the control plane. With this release, there is no longer a need for an associate site to onboard, manage, and offboard user accounts for a Chameleon associate site--you can now set up a CHI-in-a-Box site and any user already tied to a Chameleon allocation can simply use your site like one of our core sites, e.g. CHI@TACC or CHI@UC. Thanks to those who have reached out and helped shape CHI-in-a-Box to where it is today! Whether you're interested in setting up a Chameleon associate site via CHI-in-a-Box (in which case, do read </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2021/02/19/setting-associate-site-interview-northwestern-university/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">this interview with Northwestern University</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, who set up the first associate site), or you want to use the infrastructure in a more independent use-case, </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/user/help/ticket/new/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">please let us know</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">!</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Interactive Chameleon User Guide!</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> We've been watching how more people are leveraging notebooks for teaching and learning and decided to convert some of our existing documentation and tutorials into an interactive form, via Jupyter notebooks. The first module we're publishing covers several of the advanced networking capabilities, such as </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_sdn.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">BYOC (Bring Your Own Controller)</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> and creating per-experiment </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/networks/networks_vlan.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">isolated layer-2 networks</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. You can find it on, you guessed it, </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/experiment/share/33" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Trovi</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">! We'll be building this out in the future with more examples and modules. Most examples leverage the </span><a href="https://python-chi.readthedocs.io/en/latest/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Python API</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> to Chameleon </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2021/02/02/chameleon-changelog-january-2021/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">we announced last month</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> and can hopefully give you more ideas about what is possible on the testbed.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Load balancer and orchestration cloud services now available at KVM@TACC! </span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">We have deployed and configured two additional powerful capabilities at the </span><a href="https://kvm.tacc.chameleoncloud.org/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">KVM site</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. All users now can configure </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/kvm.html#load-balancer-as-a-service" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">load balancers</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> across their KVM instances. HTTP, TCP, and UDP load balancers are supported, and you can configure both health checks and the host selection algorithm. This is a commonly-found technology in commercial cloud deployments and we hope it is useful both in your experimentation and teaching. Additionally, we have added support for </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/complex.html" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">orchestration</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> at KVM. As on our bare metal clouds, orchestration on KVM allows you to conduct a group of interrelated pieces of cloud infrastructure as one unit. Like the load balancing capability, it is a feature shared by many cloud infrastructures and thus can be an interesting teaching aid. Over time we will also be working to adapt our existing complex appliances such that they can be deployed on KVM--this can make it easier to deploy virtual </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/25/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">NFS</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> and </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/33/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">MPI</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> clusters, for example. Stay tuned!</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">A "lighter" form of publishing experiment artifacts on Trovi.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> Trovi, Chameleon's sharing platform, already supports publishing your Jupyter notebook-based experiments to </span><a href="https://zenodo.org/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Zenodo</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">, a service that provides long term storage and a durable DOI, which can be cited. Sometimes, though, you just want to share something more widely without formally publishing in this way. This is now possible on Trovi: in the "Share" menu, there is a new option to publish without a DOI. Artifacts published in this way can be discovered and launched by any Chameleon user, but are not additionally stored on Zenodo. </span><a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/sharing.html#adjusting-sharing-settings" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">Read more in our docs</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">.</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Ubuntu 20.04 appliance now available.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> Ubuntu 20.04 LTS was released last year and is </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/95/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">now officially supported as a Chameleon base image</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">. We also released an </span><a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/appliances/96/" style="text-decoration: none;"><span style="">additional CUDA variant</span></a><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> of the image with the CUDA 11 toolkit preinstalled. Enjoy!</span></p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"> </p>

<p dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-top: 0pt; margin-bottom: 0pt;"><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 700; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;">Regular care and feeding of Chameleon.</span><span style="font-size: 11pt; font-family: Arial; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre; white-space: pre-wrap;"> Aside from the above, we spent some time tracking down issues we sometimes see reported. Notably, there were issues with Jupyter notebooks breaking after being logged in for a while. Let us just say that we talked to Jupyter about this, and perverse incentives, and a few other philosophical concepts, and he's fortunately come around on this matter.</span></p>

<p>We are thrilled to announce Chameleon's significant presence at the upcoming <a href="https://sc24.supercomputing.org/">SC24 conference</a> in Atlanta, Georgia, from November 17-20, 2024. This year, we have an exciting lineup of events and activities that showcase our commitment to advancing high-performance computing research and education.</p>

<p><a href="https://sc24.conference-program.com/presentation/?id=tut157&amp;sess=sess416">Link to Tutorial</a></p>

<h2>Workshop: Practical Reproducibility in HPC</h2>

<p><a href="https://reproduciblehpc.org">Link to Workshop</a></p>

<p>Join us on <strong>Monday, November 18</strong>, for our Community Workshop on Practical Reproducibility in HPC. The workshop aims to advance the concept of practical reproducibility in HPC - a practice where reproducing results becomes a mainstream method of scientific exploration. We will provide a forum for debate on the unique challenges as well as tools, services, and approaches that best support reproducibility in HPC and systems science, concluding with a comprehensive report that captures the community's collective knowledge and recommendations.</p>

<ul>
	<li>Keynotes from Dr. Kate Keahey (Argonne) on Chameleon 4 and Dr. Torsten Hoefler (ETH Zürich) on performance reproducibility</li>
	<li>Sessions chaired by Dr. Sascha Hunold (SC24 Reproducibility Chair) and Dr. Tanu Malik (former SC21 Reproducibility Chair)</li>
	<li>10 presentations from researchers at institutions worldwide</li>
	<li>Networking opportunities and rooftop happy hour</li>
</ul>

<h2>Birds of a Feather: Evolving Practical Reproducibility in HPC: Lessons from the Past, Strategies for the Future</h2>

<p><a href="https://sc24.conference-program.com/presentation/?id=bof166&amp;sess=sess589">Link to Birds of a Feather Session</a></p>

<p>On <strong>Wednesday, November 20</strong>, we're hosting a Birds of a Feather session with Sascha Hunold, Tanu Malik, Dan Katz, and Jay Lofstead. Combining insights from former SC Reproducibility Initiative chairs, thought leaders, and the SC24 community, this BoF aims to sharpen our focus on practical next steps to improve reproducibility in the HPC community. Addressing this field's unique challenges and opportunities, we’ll define practical reproducibility through interactive discussions, building on and contrasting it with computational reproducibility, and examine its real-world applications and constraints. We’ll explore the evolution of HPC reproducibility initiatives, comparing past and present approaches. We’ll also investigate the central role that specialized platforms play in enabling artifact sharing, evaluation, and usage.</p>

<h2>Chameleon Integration with SCinet and FABRIC</h2>

<p>SC24 features an advanced networking testbed (SCinet), with high-performance national and international extensions. A traveling FABRIC rack, implemented at the Ciena booth, will be available as a resource for demonstrations and experiments, including those presented through the Network Research Exhibition (NRE) program. Of particular interest to the Chameleon community is the integration of FABRIC with the Chameleon Cloud Testbed. This integration means that <strong>Chameleon resources will be available to experimenters and demonstrators at SC24</strong>, opening up new possibilities for research and collaboration.</p>

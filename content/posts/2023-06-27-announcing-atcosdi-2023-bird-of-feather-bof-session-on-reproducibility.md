<p><img src="https://chameleoncloud.org/media/filer_public/1e/fa/1efa66b8-3267-4287-82d5-35af0fb979a1/repro_bof.png" style="width: 624px; height: 468px;"></p>

<p>We are happy to announce that the Chameleon team is coming to USENIX ATC/OSDI 2023! We will host two Bird-of-Feather (BoF) sessions on practical reproducibility of one hour each. They will be held on Monday, July 10th and  Tuesday, July 11th in the 7-10PM time slot with more specific time and place to be announced at the event. </p>

<p> </p>

<p>Attendees will learn about how to package their experiments on the bare metal reconfigurable <a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/">Chameleon </a>open platform specifically configured to support computer science research. We will also demonstrate how to use <a href="https://chameleoncloud.readthedocs.io/en/latest/technical/sharing.html">Trovi</a>, a repository of digital experiments represented as <i>compute capsules</i>, i.e., integrating computation and hardware. During this hackathon session, audience can choose various reproducing/packaging activities, from the simple to the complex:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Boot up existing<a href="https://www.chameleoncloud.org/blog/2022/09/26/experiment-patterns-making-complex-experiments-easy/"> base experiment setups/patterns</a>.</li>
	<li>Boot up basic storage research experiment setups (e.g., RAID, NFS, PM, RDMA, and others TBA)</li>
	<li>Reproduce various <a href="https://chameleoncloud.org/experiment/share/">existing experiments</a> that have been packaged on Chameleon Trovi</li>
	<li>Package a new experiment from a published paper or an existing benchmark
	<ul>
		<li>If you’re a ATC/OSDI author in the last three years, we encourage you to try packaging your experiments on Chameleon – we will help!</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li>Package a new experiment related to your ongoing research</li>
</ul>

<p>After the event, participants can share their packaged experiments to their own community so that other researchers can reproduce their experiments (and potentially cite their work). We would also love to interview you about your experience and share it with others via our blog!</p>
